Oxtail with Broad Beans!

Description

This is one of my favorite meals to make on special occasions. This dish shows people who aren't familiar with Jamiacan quisine just how delicious and complex it can be(and from cheap ingredients too!).

Usually oxtail is a labour of love and takes time to make properly but with the use of a pressure cooker that won't be necessary. The prep time should be 30 minutes with a cook time of roughly 45 minutes. This recipe will serve 4 people.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 green onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger root(optional)
  • 1 scotch bonnet chile pepper, chopped(optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or maggi if you have it)
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 cup canned fava beans, drained
  • 1 teaspoon whole allspice berries
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water

Steps

  1. Toss the oxtail with the onion, green onion, garlic, ginger, chile pepper, soy sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Once up to temp, place the oxtail in the skillet and cook until the oxtail has browned all over. This should take about 10 minutes.
  4. Place into a pressure cooker, and pour in 1 1/2 cup water. Cook at pressure for 25 minutes, then remove from heat, and remove the lid according to manufacturer's directions
  5. Add the fava beans and allspice berries, and bring to a simmer over medium-high heat. Dissolve the cornstarch in 2 tablespoons water, and stir into the simmering oxtail.
  6. Cook and stir a few minutes until the sauce has thickened, and the beans are tender.
  7. Serve while hot
