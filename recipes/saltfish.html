Saltfish Fritters!

Description

This is an easy dish to start with for anyone getting in Jamiacan quisine. Fritters make a great breakfast time meal and are good for a snack throughout the day. Anytime I smell these on the stovetop I feel like I'm back on the island!

This meal is quick to make an rewarding for the time put in. The prep time is about an hour with about a 20 minute cook time. This recipe will make 24 small fritters which will be enough for 6 people(Maybe less if you fall in love with them!).

Ingredients

  • 6 ounces dried salted cod fish
  • cold water, to cover
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Soak the cod in cold water overnight to rehydrate it and remove excess salt
  2. Remove any bones and skin
  3. Sift flour, baking powder, and pepper into a large bowl. Add the diced tomatoes, green onions, and flaked cod. Pour in 1/2 cup of water and stri until everything is blended.
  4. Heat 1/4 inch of oil in a large heavy skillet over medium heat. When the oil is hot, drop rounded spoonfuls of batter into the skillet. fry on each side until golden brown and crisp, about 5 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.
